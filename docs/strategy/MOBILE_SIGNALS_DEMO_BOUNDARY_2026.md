# Mobile Signals — Demo Boundary (2026)

**Repository role:** Non-authoritative public demonstration  
**Canonical source:** `jussray/Sekret-Bip/docs/strategy/MOBILE_DEV_SIGNALS_2026.md`  
**Research date:** 2026-07-13

This repository may demonstrate product ideas. It must not become the source of truth for Se’kret Bip architecture, privacy contracts, safety behavior, backend state, or release readiness.

## Ten trends and what the demo may safely show

1. **Local-first continuity — VERIFIED**  
   Show an offline-state indicator and a fictional local draft. Do not imply real encrypted sync exists here.

2. **Narrow on-device AI — VERIFIED direction**  
   Show a labeled concept card. Do not claim device inference or private processing unless implemented and tested in the production repository.

3. **Hybrid model routing — VERIFIED direction**  
   Explain local/deterministic/server routing visually. Do not execute privileged model calls from the public client.

4. **Privacy-aware agents remain weak — VERIFIED**  
   Demonstrate explicit permission previews and blocked private surfaces rather than broad phone control.

5. **Consent as runtime state — VERIFIED requirement**  
   Show fictional share, revoke, and unlink states. Do not present the demo as evidence that production RLS or revocation works.

6. **Federated and private analytics — VERIFIED research direction**  
   Explain the concept only. Do not collect teen behavioral data for experimentation.

7. **Mobile supply-chain evidence — VERIFIED need**  
   Display a sample release-evidence card using fake data. Do not call it a real SBOM or security audit.

8. **Relationship-scoped identity — PARTIAL consumer pattern**  
   Demonstrate separate teen and parent roles without shared credentials or real account data.

9. **Signed OTA and rollback proof — VERIFIED engineering need**  
   Show the release-state model, not a fake “deployed successfully” badge.

10. **Low-data and interruption-aware design — VERIFIED**  
    Keep the demo usable without media-heavy assets, and label degraded states honestly.

## Demo product ideas

- A privacy-boundary walkthrough.
- A fictional release-evidence timeline.
- Offline and degraded-mode UI states.
- Permission previews before sample sharing.
- Accessible, high-contrast, reduced-motion controls.
- A “concept / integrated / verified / released” status legend.

## Prohibited claims

- Production-ready.
- Clinically safe or therapeutic.
- End-to-end encrypted unless proven in production.
- Parent/teen isolation proven.
- Durable AI memory implemented.
- Real on-device AI implemented.
- Live safety escalation implemented.

## Rule

When the demo and production repository differ, the production repository, implementation ledger, tests, and deployed evidence win. A pretty prototype is still a prototype, even when the gradients are emotionally persuasive.
